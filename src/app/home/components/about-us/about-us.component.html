About the Hackathon

What are
hackathons?

A hackathon is a tech competition where inventors, programmers, designers, and industry experts collaborate with each other to solve a problem. It is fun, career-focused and highly competitive. Attendees work continuously and often overnight for days to win the challenge as they battle against the clock and other teams.

Why participate?

Hackathons are a perfect opportunity for collaborations between individuals and organisations across all sectors. It is also a great opportunity to showcase your talent and skills in front of a wide number of employers and recruiters. Attendees will also receive extensive coaching and mentoring sessions from top industry experts, leaders and scholars at these events.

What experience do I need to attend?

While experience in the front end, back end and database programming is a huge plus, teams will also need people with strong skills in Marketing, Graphic designing, business analysis, pitching and presentation as well as people management, etc So, if you are an expert in any of these skills, BIngo!.

Overview

On the day of the hackathon, the facilitators will introduce the theme and the problems. Attendees will then be given a short period to brainstorm and come up with ideas. Once this time is up, those who wish to pitch their ideas will be given 180 seconds to do so. Not all attendees are required to pitch ideas.

After the pitches are presented, individuals with the best ideas will be selected to advance to the next level as team leaders. The team leaders will then announce the skills they are looking for in their teams to build a prototype of the solution they have pitched successfully. The skills required in each group vary from one another. These skills, however, are usually as follows.

  • Project managers
  • Business Analysts
  • UI/UX Designers
  • Front End/ Back End Devs
  • Database Administrators
  • Marketers, etc.

Once the teams are formed, they move on to the development stage from Day 2.

Full schedules of the hackathon workshops, seminars, talks, and coaching and mentoring sessions will be published closer to the event date.
